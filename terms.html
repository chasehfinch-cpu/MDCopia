Terms of Submission

What submitting your practice information to MDCopia means, and what every buyer is contractually obligated to do.

1. What You Are Authorizing

By submitting, you authorize MDCopia to share your practice information, valuation range, and contact details with qualified, vetted buyers (private equity firms, hospital systems, MSOs/DSOs, and independent acquirers).

Your information will only be shared after a buyer has been vetted by MDCopia and has signed the MDCopia Buyer Agreement (see Section 3).

You may withdraw your authorization at any time by emailing hello@mdcopia.com. Upon withdrawal, MDCopia will cease sharing your information with new buyers. Buyers who have already received your information will be notified that the lead has been withdrawn.

2. How MDCopia Handles Your Data

What we store: Your practice details, contact information, and valuation range are stored in a secured Google Workspace environment accessible only to MDCopia principals.

What we share with buyers: Your practice name, location, specialty, key financial metrics, valuation range, and contact information. Your payer mix detail, NPI, and additional notes are used internally to produce the valuation but are NOT shared with buyers unless you explicitly authorize it.

What we never share: Your data is never sold, rented, or traded to third parties. MDCopia does not share your information with marketing companies, data brokers, or any entity other than a vetted buyer who has signed the Buyer Agreement.

How long we keep it: Your data is retained for the duration of your engagement with MDCopia plus 12 months after your last interaction. You may request complete deletion at any time.

No tracking: MDCopia does not use cookies, tracking pixels, analytics, or device fingerprinting on any page of this site.

3. The Buyer's Agreement

Every buyer who receives a lead from MDCopia has signed the following agreement:

Range obligation. The buyer agrees to initiate negotiations with the seller at or within the valuation range provided by MDCopia. This obligation applies to the opening position of negotiations, not the final transaction price.

Good faith. The buyer agrees to negotiate in good faith and to complete reasonable due diligence before declining to proceed.

Confidentiality. The buyer agrees to keep the seller's information confidential and to use it solely for the purpose of evaluating and potentially acquiring the seller's practice.

Enforcement. Buyers who consistently open negotiations below the MDCopia-provided range, or who misuse seller information, will be permanently removed from the MDCopia marketplace and will forfeit any unused lead credits.

This agreement protects you. It ensures that any buyer who contacts you has committed, in writing, to respect the valuation established by data.

4. What MDCopia Is Not

MDCopia is not a licensed business broker. MDCopia does not represent either party in negotiations, does not negotiate on your behalf, and does not guarantee that a transaction will close. The valuation range provided is an indicative estimate, not a formal appraisal, fairness opinion, or investment advice.
