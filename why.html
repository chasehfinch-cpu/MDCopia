The Playing Field Should Be Level.

MDCopia is the only physician practice marketplace where buyers and sellers start from the same valuation.

For Practice Owners

Your Practice. Your Price. Your Decision.

The traditional path is expensive before you ever know what you have. A boutique M&A advisor will quote you $25,000 to $75,000 for an upfront practice valuation memo. A regional brokerage will charge a 3%–10% transaction commission at close — on a $5M practice, that's $150,000–$500,000 out of your proceeds. A Big 4 healthcare-advisory engagement can run $75,000–$150,000+ for a formal opinion, with months of due diligence before you have a number to share with anyone.

For most physician owners, those costs are prohibitive enough that they end up negotiating with the first PE platform that calls them. The buyer has a dedicated M&A team, comparable-transaction data, and a target multiple. The seller has a gut feel and a tax accountant. The information asymmetry is structural, and it routinely costs physicians hundreds of thousands of dollars on the deal that should be the largest financial event of their career.

MDCopia closes the gap. Free.

We produce an independent, defensible valuation range using public utilization data (CMS Medicare, NPPES), comparable transaction analyses (FOCUS, Scope, First Page Sage, Levin), specialty-specific multipliers calibrated against published deal data, and local market adjustments (BLS, Census, CMS GPCI). The range is shared with both the seller and the buyer before negotiations begin. The buyer contractually agrees to start negotiations within this range — not to close there, but to start there.

You pay nothing. No upfront fee. No success commission. No retainer. You see your valuation before you decide whether to enter the marketplace, and you can withdraw at any time.

For Buyers

Better Leads. Realistic Expectations. Faster Closes.

The problem for buyers: Cold outreach to physician practices has a low hit rate. When it does work, the physician often has unrealistic price expectations (too high or too low), which wastes months of due diligence.

The MDCopia solution: Every lead comes pre-qualified with a data-driven valuation range. The physician has already seen and accepted this range as the starting point for negotiations. This eliminates the most common reason deals fall apart: misaligned expectations on day one.

What you receive: Seller contact information, the MDCopia valuation memo (including methodology and data sources), and all available market research for the practice's geography and specialty.

Our Methodology

Data-Driven. Locally Calibrated.

The MDCopia Proprietary Engine draws on the most authoritative public datasets in U.S. healthcare M&A, including: the NPPES NPI Registry; CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners utilization and payment data; the CMS Geographic Practice Cost Index (GPCI); BLS Occupational Employment & Wage Statistics (OEWS); the BLS Consumer Price Index by Metropolitan Area; U.S. Census Bureau ACS demographic data; MGMA DataDive and the AMA Physician Practice Benchmark Survey for payer mix and benchmarks; KFF State Health Facts for state-level Medicaid expansion and coverage data; and published transaction analyses from FOCUS Investment Banking, Scope Research, First Page Sage, Levin Associates, HealthValue Group, and SovDoc. These inputs feed proprietary specialty-specific multiplier curves and geographic adjustment factors that are continuously refined using 12-month post-transaction outcome data from completed deals on the platform.
