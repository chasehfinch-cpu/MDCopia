For Practice Owners

Your Practice. Your Price. Your Decision.

The traditional path is expensive before you ever know what your practice is worth. Most physicians end up negotiating with the first PE platform that calls them, with no independent number to anchor on.

  • Boutique M&A advisor: typically $25,000 to $75,000 for an upfront practice valuation memo.
  • Regional brokerage commission: 3% to 10% of transaction value at close. On a $5M practice that is $150,000 to $500,000 out of your proceeds.
  • Big 4 healthcare-advisory opinion: $75,000 to $150,000+, with months of due diligence before you have a number you can share.
  • The hidden cost of asymmetry: the buyer has a dedicated M&A team and target multiples. You have a gut feel. That gap routinely costs physicians hundreds of thousands of dollars on what should be the largest financial event of their career.
  • MDCopia is free for sellers. No upfront fee, no success commission, no retainer. The buyer contractually agrees to begin negotiations inside your range, and you can withdraw at any time.

For Buyers

Better Leads. Realistic Expectations. Faster Closes.

The problem for buyers: Cold outreach to physician practices has a low hit rate. When it does work, the physician often has unrealistic price expectations (too high or too low), which wastes months of due diligence.

The MDCopia solution: Every lead comes pre-qualified with a data-driven valuation range. The physician has already seen and accepted this range as the starting point for negotiations. This eliminates the most common reason deals fall apart: misaligned expectations on day one.

What you receive: Seller contact information, the MDCopia valuation memo (including methodology and data sources), and all available market research for the practice's geography and specialty.

Our Methodology

Data-Driven. Locally Calibrated.

The MDCopia Proprietary Engine draws on the most authoritative public datasets in U.S. healthcare M&A. Every valuation is built from these inputs:

  • NPPES NPI Registry for provider identity, taxonomy, and practice age.
  • CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners for per-NPI utilization and Medicare allowed amounts.
  • CMS Geographic Practice Cost Index (GPCI) for healthcare-specific geographic cost adjustment.
  • BLS Occupational Employment & Wage Statistics (OEWS) for physician-compensation benchmarks by specialty and state.
  • BLS Consumer Price Index by Metropolitan Area for cost-of-living context.
  • U.S. Census Bureau ACS for population, age mix, growth, and median income.
  • MGMA DataDive and the AMA Physician Practice Benchmark Survey for payer-mix benchmarks.
  • KFF State Health Facts for state-level Medicaid expansion and coverage.
  • Published transaction analyses from FOCUS Investment Banking, Scope Research, First Page Sage, Levin Associates, HealthValue Group, and SovDoc for calibration of revenue and EBITDA multiples.

These inputs feed proprietary specialty-specific multiplier curves and geographic adjustment factors. The engine is continuously refined using 12-month post-transaction outcome data from completed deals on the platform.
