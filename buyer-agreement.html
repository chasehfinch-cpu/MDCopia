MDCopia Buyer Agreement

Effective date: May 1, 2026  ·  Version: 1.0  ·  Operator: MDCopia, LLC, a U.S. limited liability company

This Buyer Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between MDCopia, LLC ("MDCopia," "we," "us," or "our") and the buying party identified by the email and organization on a paid MDCopia invoice ("Buyer," "you," or "your"). This Agreement governs your access to and use of seller information and valuation memos provided by MDCopia (collectively, "Leads").

Acceptance by payment. Your payment of any MDCopia invoice constitutes your binding acceptance of every term of this Agreement. If you do not agree, do not pay the invoice and do not access any Lead delivered by MDCopia.

1. Definitions

1.1 "Seller" means a physician practice owner, officer, or authorized agent who has submitted practice information to MDCopia for valuation and marketplace purposes and who has authorized MDCopia, in writing, to share Practice Information with vetted Buyers.
1.2 "Practice Information" means the information disclosed by MDCopia to Buyer in connection with a Lead, including without limitation practice name, location, specialty, financial summary metrics (annual revenue, annual visits, sites, real estate status, sale timeline), the MDCopia Valuation Output, and Seller's contact email.
1.3 "Valuation Output" means the indicative valuation range, methodology summary, and supporting analysis produced by the MDCopia Proprietary Engine for a particular Seller.
1.4 "Lead" means a single, defined disclosure of Practice Information from MDCopia to Buyer for a specified Seller, for which Buyer has paid MDCopia a per-Lead fee.
1.5 "Negotiation Range" means the indicative enterprise value range published in the Valuation Output for the Seller covered by a Lead.

2. Eligibility & Vetting

2.1 Buyer represents that it is (a) a private equity firm, hospital system, management services organization (MSO), dental services organization (DSO), holding company, family office, or other bona fide acquirer of physician medical practices; (b) duly organized and in good standing in its jurisdiction of formation; and (c) authorized to enter into and perform this Agreement.
2.2 MDCopia retains sole and absolute discretion to vet, approve, suspend, or remove any Buyer at any time. MDCopia may refuse to deliver Leads to a Buyer for any reason or for no reason.
2.3 Buyer shall, upon MDCopia's reasonable request, provide documentation supporting its eligibility, including organizational documents, evidence of capital availability, and prior physician-practice transaction history.

3. Lead Delivery & Fees

3.1 MDCopia delivers each Lead by email to the address on Buyer's invoice. Each Lead corresponds to one Seller and one paid invoice.
3.2 The fee for each Lead is set forth on the corresponding MDCopia invoice. Fees are due and payable in full prior to MDCopia's release of any Practice Information. Buyer's payment of an invoice constitutes Buyer's acceptance of this Agreement with respect to all Leads now or hereafter delivered to Buyer by MDCopia.
3.3 All fees are non-refundable except as expressly provided in Section 9.
3.4 MDCopia is compensated solely on a per-Lead basis. MDCopia does not collect a transaction commission, success fee, or any contingent payment from any party at the close of a transaction between Buyer and Seller.

4. Range Obligation

4.1 Buyer shall initiate negotiations with the Seller named on a Lead at or within the Negotiation Range published in that Lead's Valuation Output. This obligation applies to Buyer's opening offer or letter of intent.
4.2 The Range Obligation applies to Buyer's opening position only. Final transaction value is subject to negotiation, due diligence, deal structure, and the parties' mutual agreement.
4.3 A "below-range opening" is any initial written offer, term sheet, or letter of intent that proposes consideration to Seller below the low end of the Negotiation Range, measured on an apples-to-apples basis (i.e., normalizing for cash, equity, earn-out, real estate, and any working-capital adjustments).
4.4 Buyer who issues a below-range opening on three (3) or more Leads in any rolling twelve-month period shall be deemed in material breach of this Agreement and may be removed from the MDCopia marketplace at MDCopia's sole discretion, with forfeiture of any unused Lead credits.

5. Good-Faith Negotiation

5.1 Buyer shall negotiate with each Seller in good faith and shall complete reasonable due diligence before declining to proceed with a transaction.
5.2 Buyer shall not use a Lead as a market-intelligence-only tool. Buyer represents that each Lead is acquired with bona fide intent to evaluate the Seller as an acquisition target.
5.3 Buyer shall communicate a decision to Seller (whether to proceed, decline, or remain in diligence) within sixty (60) days of Buyer's receipt of the Lead, except where the parties have mutually agreed in writing to extend that period.

6. Confidentiality

6.1 Buyer shall keep all Practice Information strictly confidential and shall use it solely for the purpose of evaluating and potentially acquiring the Seller's practice.
6.2 Buyer shall restrict access to Practice Information on a need-to-know basis within the Buyer organization, and shall ensure that each individual with access is bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those of this Section 6.
6.3 Buyer shall not share, transfer, sublicense, or re-disclose Practice Information to any third party (including affiliates, advisors, lenders, or co-investors) without (a) MDCopia's prior written consent and (b) Seller's prior written consent.
6.4 Upon any decision by Buyer not to proceed with a Seller, Buyer shall, within thirty (30) days, return or destroy all Practice Information in its possession (including copies, derivatives, and notes) and shall provide written certification of such destruction upon MDCopia's request.
6.5 The confidentiality and non-use obligations of this Section 6 shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years.

7. No Re-Disclosure of Aggregated Data

Buyer may not aggregate Practice Information from multiple Leads into market-research products, deal-pipeline reports, investor decks, or any other deliverable shared outside Buyer's internal acquisition team without MDCopia's prior written consent.

8. MDCopia's Role and Limitations

8.1 MDCopia is an information-services and marketplace operator. MDCopia is not a licensed business broker, investment advisor, registered representative, attorney, or accountant.
8.2 MDCopia does not represent either Buyer or Seller in any negotiation. MDCopia does not negotiate price, structure, or terms on behalf of either party.
8.3 The Valuation Output is an indicative range produced by MDCopia's proprietary engine. It is not a formal appraisal, fairness opinion, audit, or investment advice and does not satisfy regulatory, fiduciary, or fairness standards required for any board approval, lender requirement, tax filing, or court proceeding.
8.4 MDCopia makes no representation or warranty as to the accuracy or completeness of Practice Information provided by a Seller. Buyer is responsible for conducting its own due diligence prior to closing any transaction.

9. Refunds & Lead Credit

9.1 A Lead is non-refundable once Practice Information has been delivered to Buyer.
9.2 Notwithstanding Section 9.1, MDCopia will, at Buyer's election, issue either (i) a full refund of the per-Lead fee paid for the affected Lead, or (ii) a non-expiring Lead Credit in the amount of the per-Lead fee, redeemable against any future Lead delivered by MDCopia, in any of the following circumstances: (a) the Seller named on the Lead withdraws from the marketplace before Buyer has initiated contact; (b) MDCopia determines, after delivery, that the Practice Information contained material inaccuracies attributable to MDCopia (and not to Seller's representations); or (c) MDCopia removes the Seller from the marketplace for any reason within fourteen (14) days of Lead delivery.
9.3 Lead Credits are recorded by MDCopia against Buyer's account, are non-transferable, do not expire, and may be applied against any future MDCopia invoice. MDCopia makes no representation as to the timing, availability, or specialty/geography of any future Lead, and the issuance of a Lead Credit does not obligate MDCopia to deliver any specific subsequent Lead.

10. Term & Termination

10.1 This Agreement is effective on the date of Buyer's first invoice payment and continues in effect for so long as Buyer holds an active relationship with MDCopia.
10.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the other.
10.3 MDCopia may terminate this Agreement immediately, without notice, upon Buyer's material breach (including, without limitation, breach of Sections 4, 5, 6, or 7), upon Buyer's insolvency or bankruptcy filing, or upon any conduct by Buyer that MDCopia reasonably determines compromises the integrity of the marketplace.
10.4 Sections 4 (with respect to any Lead already delivered), 5.3, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, and 16 shall survive termination.

11. Liquidated Damages for Confidentiality Breach

Because actual damages from a breach of Section 6 (Confidentiality) or Section 7 (No Re-Disclosure of Aggregated Data) would be difficult to quantify, the parties agree that, in lieu of actual damages and as a reasonable forecast of just compensation, Buyer shall pay MDCopia liquidated damages of twenty-five thousand U.S. dollars ($25,000) per Lead as to which a breach occurs. This provision is in addition to, and not in lieu of, any equitable relief available under Section 14.

12. Disclaimers

12.1 NO WARRANTY ON LEADS. Practice Information is provided "AS IS" and "AS AVAILABLE." MDCopia disclaims all warranties, express or implied, including warranties of accuracy, completeness, merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, and non-infringement.
12.2 NO TRANSACTION GUARANTEE. MDCopia does not guarantee that any Lead will result in a transaction, that any Seller will accept Buyer's offer, or that any transaction will close.

13. Limitation of Liability

13.1 To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, in no event shall MDCopia, its members, officers, employees, contractors, or agents be liable to Buyer for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, exemplary, or punitive damages, including but not limited to lost profits, lost goodwill, lost business opportunities, or diminished transaction value, arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, regardless of the theory of liability.
13.2 MDCopia's total cumulative liability to Buyer for all claims arising under or relating to this Agreement shall not exceed the aggregate fees paid by Buyer to MDCopia in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

14. Indemnification & Equitable Relief

14.1 Buyer shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless MDCopia, its members, officers, employees, contractors, and agents from and against any third-party claims, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of (a) Buyer's breach of this Agreement; (b) Buyer's misuse or unauthorized disclosure of Practice Information; or (c) Buyer's transaction or attempted transaction with any Seller.
14.2 Buyer acknowledges that any breach of Section 6 (Confidentiality) or Section 7 (No Re-Disclosure of Aggregated Data) would cause MDCopia irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. MDCopia shall therefore be entitled to seek injunctive and other equitable relief without the requirement of posting bond.

15. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

15.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-law provisions.
15.2 Any dispute arising under or relating to this Agreement shall first be submitted to confidential, good-faith negotiation between the parties for thirty (30) days.
15.3 Disputes not resolved through negotiation shall be finally resolved by binding arbitration administered by JAMS under its Comprehensive Arbitration Rules and Procedures, conducted in Wilmington, Delaware, before a single arbitrator. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.
15.4 Notwithstanding the foregoing, MDCopia may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in the state or federal courts located in Delaware to enforce Sections 6, 7, 11, or 14.

16. General

16.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous communications, whether oral or written.
16.2 Amendments. MDCopia may amend this Agreement by posting an updated version at www.mdcopia.com/buyer-agreement.html and notifying Buyer by email. Buyer's continued payment of invoices after the effective date of an amendment constitutes acceptance of the amended Agreement.
16.3 Assignment. Buyer may not assign or transfer this Agreement without MDCopia's prior written consent. MDCopia may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.
16.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be reformed to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the parties' original intent.
16.5 Notices. Notices to MDCopia shall be sent to sales@mdcopia.com. Notices to Buyer shall be sent to the email address on Buyer's most recent paid invoice.
ACCEPTANCE. By paying any MDCopia invoice on or after the Effective Date, Buyer signifies its agreement to be bound by every term and condition of this Buyer Agreement.
