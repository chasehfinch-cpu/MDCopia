Our Methodology

Data-driven. Locally calibrated.

The MDCopia Proprietary Engine draws on the most authoritative public datasets in U.S. healthcare M&A. Every valuation is built from these inputs. The list below shows what we extract from each source. The multipliers and weighting that combine them are proprietary to MDCopia.

NPPES NPI Registry

Your enumeration date, primary taxonomy code, organizational affiliations, and authorized-official information.

CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners

Total Medicare allowed amount, beneficiary count, average risk score, and chronic-condition prevalence for your most recent reporting year.

CMS Geographic Practice Cost Index (GPCI)

The composite of work, practice expense, and malpractice indices for your specific Medicare locality.

BLS Occupational Employment & Wage Statistics

The median physician compensation for your specialty in your state.

BLS Consumer Price Index by Metro Area

Your metropolitan area's cost-of-living index relative to the U.S. national average.

U.S. Census Bureau ACS

Total population, age-65+ share, 5-year population growth, and median household income for your state.

MGMA & AMA Benchmarks

Published payer-mix benchmarks for your specialty: Medicare share, Medicaid share, commercial share, self-pay share.

KFF State Health Facts

Your state's Medicaid expansion status and senior-population share.

Published Transaction Analyses

Observed revenue and EBITDA multiples for recent transactions in your specialty band, sourced from FOCUS Investment Banking, Scope Research, First Page Sage, Levin Associates, HealthValue Group, and SovDoc.

These inputs feed proprietary specialty-specific multiplier curves and geographic adjustment factors. The engine is continuously refined using 12-month post-transaction outcome data from completed deals on the platform.
