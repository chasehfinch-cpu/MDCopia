MDCopia Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 1, 2026  ·  Version: 1.0  ·  Operator: MDCopia, LLC, a U.S. limited liability company

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how MDCopia, LLC ("MDCopia," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, retains, discloses, and protects information from users of www.mdcopia.com and any related properties (collectively, the "Platform"). By using the Platform or submitting information to MDCopia, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy.

1. Definitions

1.1 "Seller" means a physician, owner, officer, or authorized agent of a physician medical practice who submits practice information to MDCopia for valuation or marketplace purposes.
1.2 "Buyer" means a private equity firm, hospital system, management services organization (MSO), dental services organization (DSO), or other acquirer that has been vetted by MDCopia and has executed the MDCopia Buyer Agreement.
1.3 "Practice Information" means the data fields a Seller provides through the valuation form, including without limitation practice name, specialty, city, state, National Provider Identifier (NPI) (optional), annual revenue, annual visits, number of sites, real estate status, sale timeline, and contact email.
1.4 "Valuation Output" means the indicative valuation range, methodology summary, key factors, and data-source list produced by the MDCopia Proprietary Engine in response to a Seller's submission.
1.5 "Personal Information" means information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular individual or household.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information you provide. We collect Practice Information when you complete the valuation form, your email address when you choose to receive your full report, the 6-digit code you enter to verify that email address, and any inbound correspondence you send to MDCopia.
2.2 Information derived from public records. If you supply an NPI, we query the publicly available NPPES NPI Registry and the CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners dataset to enrich your valuation. The enriched data is stored alongside your submission record and is treated with the same confidentiality protections as Practice Information.
2.3 Information we do NOT collect. We do not use cookies, web beacons, tracking pixels, advertising identifiers, browser-fingerprinting techniques, or third-party analytics. We do not collect IP addresses for tracking purposes. We do not require account creation to use the valuation engine.

3. How We Use Information

3.1 Practice Information is used solely (a) to compute and deliver your Valuation Output; (b) to record your submission for the purpose of a future Buyer match, contingent on your acknowledgment of the Terms of Submission; (c) to communicate with you about your submission; and (d) to comply with applicable legal obligations.
3.2 Email addresses are used only to deliver verification codes, valuation reports, and Buyer-introduction messages, and to respond to inbound inquiries. We do not send marketing or promotional emails.
3.3 We do not use Practice Information for advertising, behavioral targeting, or aggregation into commercial datasets sold or licensed to third parties.

4. Disclosure to Third Parties

4.1 Buyers. Practice Information identified in Section 5 of the Terms of Submission may be disclosed to a Buyer who (a) has been vetted by MDCopia, (b) has executed the MDCopia Buyer Agreement (including the negotiation-range obligation), and (c) has paid MDCopia for the lead. Disclosure occurs after your submission and acknowledgment of the Terms of Submission.
4.2 Service providers. We use Google Workspace (for data storage in Google Sheets) and Resend, Inc. (for transactional email delivery). These providers act as our processors under contractual obligations restricting use of your information to the services they provide to us.
4.3 Legal compliance. We may disclose information if required by subpoena, court order, or other lawful request, or where we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect MDCopia's rights, comply with a judicial proceeding, or respond to a government request.
4.4 Business transfer. In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of substantially all of MDCopia's assets, your information may be transferred to the successor entity, subject to the protections of this Policy or a successor policy of comparable protection.
4.5 What we will never do. We will never sell, rent, trade, or otherwise commercially transfer Personal Information to data brokers, marketing firms, or any party other than as described in Sections 4.1 through 4.4.

5. Data Retention

5.1 Practice Information and Valuation Outputs are retained for the duration of your engagement with MDCopia plus twelve (12) months following your last interaction with the Platform.
5.2 Email verification codes expire thirty (30) minutes after generation and are not reusable.
5.3 Aggregated, de-identified market data derived from multiple submissions may be retained indefinitely for the purpose of refining the Proprietary Engine, provided that such data cannot reasonably be re-identified to any individual Seller.

6. Your Rights

6.1 Right to access. You may request a copy of all Practice Information we hold about you by emailing sales@mdcopia.com. We will respond within thirty (30) days.
6.2 Right to correction. You may request correction of inaccurate Practice Information at any time using the same address.
6.3 Right to deletion. You may request deletion of your Practice Information at any time. Upon receipt of a deletion request, MDCopia will remove your record from active systems within fourteen (14) days, subject to legal retention obligations and any pending Buyer engagements you have authorized.
6.4 Right to withdraw consent. You may withdraw your authorization for Buyer disclosure at any time. Withdrawal terminates new Buyer matches immediately; Buyers who have already received your information will be notified of the withdrawal.
6.5 Residents of California, Colorado, Connecticut, Virginia, Utah, and other U.S. jurisdictions with comprehensive consumer privacy laws have additional statutory rights, including the right to non-discrimination for exercising these rights. We honor those rights for all Sellers regardless of residency.

7. Information Security

7.1 Practice Information is stored in a Google Workspace environment with access restricted to MDCopia principals via single sign-on with two-factor authentication.
7.2 All data in transit between your browser and the Platform is encrypted via TLS 1.2 or later. Data in transit between the Platform and our processors is similarly encrypted.
7.3 No method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is one hundred percent secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but maintain administrative, physical, and technical safeguards reasonably designed to protect Practice Information.

8. Children's Privacy

The Platform is not directed to children under thirteen (13) and we do not knowingly collect Personal Information from children. If you believe a child has submitted information, please contact us so we can delete it.

9. International Users

The Platform is operated from and intended for use within the United States. If you access the Platform from outside the U.S., you do so on your own initiative and consent to the transfer and processing of your information in the United States, which may not have data-protection laws equivalent to those of your jurisdiction.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. The "Effective date" at the top reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be communicated by email to active Sellers at least fourteen (14) days before they take effect. Continued use of the Platform after a change indicates acceptance of the revised Policy.

11. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

11.1 This Policy is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-law provisions.
11.2 Any dispute arising under or relating to this Policy shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Delaware, and you and MDCopia consent to the personal jurisdiction of those courts.
